Bio

Have you ever wondered if you can hear dust? Nick has.

Nick Henderson’s world exists in the cracks between minor chords on felt piano and lo-fi tape ambience. On a planet where electro-post-rock-jazz exists; Nick’s love of alternate guitar tunings, introspective synth moods and all things low-end have an ever evolving place in today’s musical landscape.

Passionate about ambient electronic music and old jazz records - and obsessed with polyrhythmic death metal and indie-pop songwriting; Nick has a treasure chest of both experience and inspiration. A highly sought after bassist (upright and electric) with a jazz degree from the Sydney Conservatorium of Music, his music creation extends to guitar, singing, synthesizers and recording - with a special penchant for old digital drum machines and even older upright pianos. Have you ever wondered if you can hear dust? Nick has.

Like many multi-instrumentalists in a post/post-music world, Nick suffers from multiple-project syndrome. His latest projects include the electronic ’50 cycles’, as well as being one half of the singer-songwriter duo ‘Little Clouds’. He also composes and collaborates with prog-jazzers 'Arcing Wires’ and ‘Twirly Bender. Sideman duties include Ellen Kirkwood’s Underwards, Emily-Rose and the Wild Things and the Yutaro Okuda Quintet.

His music has seen him tour the USA, Japan, Indonesia, Netherlands, Germany and France, as well as playing alongside Grammy nominated pianist Elio VillaFranca and award winning jazz pianist Mike Nock.

Nick hopes one day that you will enjoy his music the way he enjoys listening to his favourite artists; through his Dad’s genuine 70’s vintage B & W speakers.
